Project Report Content Introduction (100–150 words)

This project was completed as part of the Digital Literacy course, where I took on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador. The aim of this project was to understand and demonstrate essential digital skills required for academic and professional success. Through five different tasks, I explored areas such as digital awareness, professional online presence, coding platforms, communication etiquette, and cybercrime safety. Each task helped me gain practical experience using real-world tools like GitHub, LinkedIn, Google Forms, and coding platforms. This project not only improved my technical knowledge but also enhanced my communication and online responsibility. Overall, it helped me understand how to use digital platforms effectively, safely, and professionally in today’s digital world.

Task 1 Write-up (150–200 words)

For this task, I created a digital literacy awareness infographic using Canva. The design focused on explaining what digital literacy means, the importance of maintaining a professional online presence, and safe internet practices for students. I included key points such as protecting personal data, using digital tools effectively, and following proper email etiquette. The visual layout was designed to be simple, attractive, and easy to understand for college students.

While creating the infographic, I explored different templates, icons, and color combinations to make the content engaging. One challenge I faced was organizing the information clearly without overcrowding the design. However, this helped me learn how to present information concisely using visuals.

This task improved my creativity and taught me how to communicate important ideas through design. It also made me realize the importance of spreading awareness about digital responsibility among students in a simple and effective way.

Task 2 Write-up (150–200 words)

In this task, I created and updated my profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and one coding-related platform. GitHub was used to build my digital portfolio and showcase my projects, while LinkedIn helped me establish a professional identity online. Each platform serves a unique purpose—GitHub for project sharing, LinkedIn for networking, and coding platforms for skill development.

On GitHub, I created a profile README that includes my name, branch, and learning goals. On LinkedIn, I added my education details and began building a professional profile. These steps helped me understand how important it is to maintain a clean and professional online presence.

In the next four years, I plan to regularly update these platforms by adding projects, certifications, and achievements. This will help me build a strong portfolio for internships and placements. Overall, this task taught me how to present myself professionally in the digital world and prepare for future career opportunities.

Task 3 Write-up (150–200 words)

For this task, I explored both coding practice and collaboration tools. I created an account on a coding platform and completed a beginner-level challenge, which helped me understand problem-solving and basic programming logic. It was a good starting point to improve my coding skills.

In addition, I created a Google Form titled “Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz” with five questions. The form included both multiple-choice and short-answer questions to test students’ knowledge. I also viewed the responses in Google Sheets, which helped me understand how data can be collected and analyzed easily.

This task showed me how useful online platforms are for learning and collaboration. Coding platforms help in improving technical skills, while tools like Google Forms make it easy to collect information and conduct quizzes. These tools will be very helpful in my academic journey for assignments, projects, and teamwork.

Task 4 Write-up (100–150 words)

In this task, I learned the importance of professional communication through email writing and social media etiquette. I drafted two formal emails—one to a professor requesting a deadline extension and another to an internship coordinator expressing interest in an opportunity. This helped me understand the correct structure, tone, and format of professional emails.

I also created a checklist of social media do’s and don’ts to promote responsible online behavior. Additionally, I analyzed a situation where poor digital communication caused problems due to unclear and unprofessional messaging.

This task taught me that clear, polite, and structured communication is essential in academic and professional environments. Following proper etiquette can improve responses and maintain a positive impression.

Task 5 Write-up (100–150 words)

In this task, I explored cybercrime awareness by studying a case related to online fraud. I learned how cybercrimes like phishing and UPI fraud occur and how easily individuals can become victims if they are not careful. One thing that surprised me was how attackers use simple tricks like fake links or messages to steal sensitive information.

I also created a prevention checklist with practical tips to stay safe online, including secure password usage and safe online transactions.

As a result of this task, I will be more cautious while clicking on unknown links and sharing personal information online. This activity helped me understand the importance of cybersecurity and encouraged me to adopt safer digital habits.

Conclusion (100 words)

Overall, this project provided valuable knowledge about digital literacy and its importance in today’s world. Each task helped me develop different skills, including design, professional communication, online presence, and cybersecurity awareness. I learned how to use various digital tools effectively and responsibly. This project also improved my confidence in using online platforms for academic and professional purposes. Most importantly, it taught me how to be a responsible digital user and how to guide others as a Digital Ambassador. These skills will be useful throughout my academic journey and future career.
