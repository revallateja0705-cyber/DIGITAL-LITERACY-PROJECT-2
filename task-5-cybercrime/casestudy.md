
Case Study: Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals use digital platforms (social media, messaging apps, or forums) to threaten, shame, or humiliate others. Unlike traditional bullying, it can occur 24/7 and reach a wide audience instantly, making it more persistent and damaging.

A real-world style example is a 2024 incident in Liverpool where a teenager was targeted after posting a video online. The bullying unfolded step-by-step:

The victim posted content expressing a personal opinion.
Other users reacted negatively and created multiple fake accounts.
Personal information (address, school details) was leaked publicly (“doxxing”).
Harassment escalated across platforms, with threats and abusive messages.
The abuse moved offline, with strangers contacting and intimidating the family.

Cyberbullying typically targets teenagers and young adults, especially those who are perceived as “different,” socially vulnerable, or outspoken online. Victims are often classmates or peers, and in many cases, the bully is someone the victim already knows.

The consequences are severe. Victims may experience anxiety, depression, fear, and social withdrawal. In extreme cases, families are forced to relocate for safety, and victims may require psychological treatment. Research also shows links to low self-esteem and even suicidal thoughts or actions in severe situations.
